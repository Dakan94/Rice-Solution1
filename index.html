Farmer Training Programs

Problem: Traditional Farming Methods

Many farmers in Tanzania's Western Zone still rely on traditional methods that limit productivity and increase vulnerability to climate change. These practices include:

  • Use of low-yielding local seed varieties
  • Manual farming with rudimentary tools
  • Rain-fed agriculture with no irrigation backup
  • Poor post-harvest handling leading to significant losses
  • Limited knowledge of modern agronomic practices

Empowering Farmers with Modern Agronomy

Our comprehensive training program is the backbone of our empowerment strategy. It transitions farmers from traditional, low-yielding methods (average ~1 t/ha) to high-efficiency, climate-smart practices.

Training Modules

Seed & Input Quality

Training on identifying and using improved seed varieties, proper fertilizer application, and pest management.

Water Management

Implementing sustainable irrigation systems and techniques for optimal water use.

Mechanization

Integrating machinery for land preparation, planting, and harvesting to increase efficiency.

Post-Harvest Techniques

Reducing losses through proper handling, drying, storage, and processing protocols.

The Dakan Mill Solution: Vertical Integration

The Problem We Solve

Farmers face low yields, climate vulnerability, significant post-harvest losses, and exploitation by middlemen due to limited market access.

1

Modern Production Foundation

We integrate improved seed varieties, sustainable irrigation systems, and full mechanization to move from manual, low-output farming to high-yield, resilient agriculture.

2

Secure Value Chain Control

We manage the process from field to market, investing in storage and processing facilities to drastically cut post-harvest losses, ensuring quality and maximizing farmer income.

3

Direct Premium Market Access

We forge direct market linkages with urban households, institutions, and retailers in high-demand zones (Mwanza, Shinyanga, Tabora, Kigoma), securing better and more stable prices than local farm-gate rates.
